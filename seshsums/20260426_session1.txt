  Implementation: --curriculum flag in train.py.
    - Stages: max_depth 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 (cumulative data at each stage)
    - Advance when test_same >= 0.99 (threshold reached) OR stalls (patience=3)
    - test_deeper fixed throughout as the generalization probe
    - LR schedule continues uninterrupted across stage transitions
    - Same baseline model: 2L 4H d128, 410K params, max_iters=10000

  Stage transitions:
    Stage 1 (depth≤1): mastered at step 200  (test_deeper=0.093)
    Stage 2 (depth≤2): mastered at step 600  (test_deeper=0.175)
    Stage 3 (depth≤3): stalled at step 3800  (test_deeper peaked ~0.228)
    Stage 4 (depth≤4): stalled at step 4800  (test_deeper=0.264)

  Final results:
    Joint training (any model size):   test_deeper = 0.173  (hard ceiling)
    Curriculum 1→2→3→4:               test_deeper = 0.264  (+53% relative)

  Interpretation:
    Progressive training lifted depth-4 generalization from 17% to 26% using the
    same model and same total parameters. Most of the gain came during stage 3
    (depth-3 data), confirming that staged exposure to intermediate complexity
    matters. The model still hasn't "solved" depth-4 generalization — 26% is well
    below ceiling — but the gap between joint and curriculum training is real and
    confirms the training distribution (not capacity) as the bottleneck.

    This is the first concrete evidence for the spec's central hypothesis: that
    developmental/progressive training order affects what the model learns.

--- Model architecture notes ---

  The model is a standard decoder-only transformer (nanoGPT style). Each Block
  contains two sublayers:
    1. CausalSelfAttention — multi-head self-attention with a causal (lower-triangular)
       mask so each token can only attend to itself and earlier tokens.
    2. MLP — two-layer feedforward with GELU activation, 4x hidden expansion.

  Why attention matters for this task:
    Negation distribution is inherently a context-sensitive, structure-tracking
    operation. To correctly rewrite -(a-(b+c)) the model needs to track the
    accumulated sign across nested parentheses — a token in the output ('+' or '-')
    depends on the sign context built up by all the enclosing neg/add/sub nodes to
    its left in the input. Attention is the mechanism that could, in principle,
    implement this: heads could learn to "look back" at relevant sign-flipping tokens
    in the input and propagate the accumulated sign forward.

    Whether the model actually uses attention this way — vs. learning shallow
    positional or bigram statistics that happen to work on depth-2 but break at
    depth-4 — is an open question, and a prime target for mechanistic interpretability
    probes (attention pattern visualization, activation patching). The 17% test_deeper
    ceiling strongly suggests the model is NOT learning a general compositional rule
    via attention, but rather surface patterns that don't compose to deeper nesting.

  next experiment. More capacity cannot substitute for exposure to deeper structure.

--- Key project context (from spec discussions) ---

  Research questions:
    - Reification: do implicit procedures become explicit reusable concepts?
    - Misconceptions: procedural errors vs. coherent wrong internal "understandings"
    - Algebraic reasoning emergence: general principles over classes
    - Bounded rationality (Simon/Gigerenzer): compact abstractions that generalize

  Training regimes (central experimental axis):
    - Joint/Random: all expression types mixed
    - Progressive/Curriculum: digits -> add/sub -> neg -> fractions/division

  Generalization probes:
    - Held-out digits (model has embeddings but never sees them in training)
    - Novel recombinations of learned sub-expressions (compositional generalization)
    - Deeper nesting (OOD depth)

  Theoretical framing:
    - Reification (Sfard, Dubinsky APOS, Tall procept)
    - Dehaene number sense (implicit magnitude foundations)
    - Simon scissors + Gigerenzer ecological rationality
